sandman's Fodder Undead System

This system is for lesser undead. This is for big hack and slash slash encounters akin to something from the Doom, Ultrakill, or Hades games. This is an alternative to making a full character sheet. This just gives the essentials:

*Fodder Undead CANNOT be stand users
Essentials
  • PB
  • Health
  • AC
  • Damage
  • Ability Scores
  • Scaling

Proficiency Bonus (PB)

The PB for a Fodder undead can range from +2 to +6. Undead with a lesser PB are an inexperienced undead, not necessarily weaker. Undead with a greater PB have spent a longer time being an undead. PB is like levels for undead. Ghoul Levels are equal to PB -1. Any ASIs are just +2 to modifiers.

Health

There are 5 archetypes for health

  • Tank: d12 - For undead you want to last for a long time
  • Wall: d10 - For undead you want to last for a while
  • Durable: d8 - For undead you want to last for a bit
  • Normal: d6 - For undead you want to last
  • Frail: d4 - For undead you want gone

The amount of hit die is equal to the undeadâ€™s PB. You can also double or even square it if needed. The equation for HP is as follows: Max Hit Dice Roll + (Hit Dice Roll per PB) + (Con * PB)

Armor Class (AC)

AC for undead is equal to 10 + PB + (The highest between: Con OR Dex OR Wis). I recommend using Con if the undead specializes in Strength or Constitution. Use Dex for if the undead specializes in Dexterity or Charisma. Use Wis if the character specializes in Wisdom or Intelligence. Head AC is regular AC but with PB added once more.

Damage

Damage can be done through Weapons, but most of the time, they use their fists and nothing else.

  • Powerful: d12
  • Brusier: d10
  • Intermediate: d8
  • Weakling: d6

Generally the higher the attack die is the lesser number of attacks. Number of attacks is equal to PB.if PB is 4 or above attack dice double and go down a tier (1d12 => 2d10, 1d10 => 2d8, etc.) with the exception of 1d6 which turns into 2d6.

Ability Scores

Saving the most complex for last. There are 15 archetypes for ability scores, each one specializing in 2 scores. The specialized scores have a +4 in one and +3 in the other while the others are +2/+1/0/-1. You are proficient in specialized scores.

  1. Guts: Strength and Constitution
  2. Force: Strength and Dexterity
  3. Strategy: Strength and Intelligence
  4. Resolve: Strength and Wisdom
  5. Dominance: Strength and Charisma
  6. Agility: Dexterity and Constitution
  7. Precision: Dexterity and Intelligence
  8. Reflex: Dexterity and Wisdom
  9. Grace: Dexterity and Charisma
  10. Fortitude: Constitution and Intelligence
  11. Conviction: Constitution and Wisdom
  12. Vitality: Constitution and Charisma
  13. Insight: Intelligence and Wisdom
  14. Cunning: Intelligence and Charisma
  15. Composure: Wisdom and Charisma

The Specializations of Ability scores is what provides the most variety in the fodder. I wouldn't recommend having more than 2 of each in each encounter unless you are using ridiculous amounts of fodder.

Scaling

Scaling is an important part of building encounters with fodder undead. The main factor in scaling with this system is the PB and Amount. I wouldn't recommend more than 20 fodder undead in one encounter. PB is equal to player level for scaling. Meaning to have the two sides equal is the following Equation:

(PB * # of fodder undead) - other levels = Total Party Levels
